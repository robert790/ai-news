1

Pick a vertical you actually understand.

Not "AI for everyone." Pick something you know about — from your work, your hobby, your family's business, your old job. The best AI products come from people who feel the pain they're solving. Write down 3 ideas. Pick the one that hurts most.

2

Talk to 10 humans in that world.

Email, message, call — doesn't matter. "Hi, I'm exploring building X. Would you give me 15 minutes?" Most won't reply. The ones who do will teach you more than any course. No building yet. Just listening.

3

Build the dumbest possible prototype.

Open ChatGPT, Claude, or DeepSeek's chat. Type your idea in. See how well it works. That's your baseline. Now try to make it 2× better with prompt engineering. Then try RAG by pasting in real documents. Get something ugly working before you make it pretty.

The whole game in one sentence: don't compete on the model. Compete on the problem you solve and how well you solve it. The model is a tool. The problem is the business.

You don't need to be Sam Altman. You don't need to be DeepSeek. You need to be a person who knows a real pain and uses existing tools well to fix it. That's a perfectly good company. That's most of the AI startups that will actually make money in the next 5 years.

Now go talk to those 10 humans. Seriously. The sooner you start, the sooner it becomes real.
