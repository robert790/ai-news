The big idea: Romania is small in absolute AI terms compared to the US or China, but it's now an official EU AI Factory host (October 2025). That puts you inside an EU-funded compute and skills ecosystem most countries don't have access to. Use it.

The big players, ranked by what matters to you.

Provider 1 · ClusterPower (Iași + nationwide)

The biggest domestic GPU-as-a-service.

What they offer: on-demand and reserved access to NVIDIA H100 GPU clusters, with InfiniBand-class networking. Built specifically for AI training and inference at scale. They sell to anyone — startups, research labs, enterprises.

Why it matters to you: if you ever want to fine-tune a real model (not just use APIs), you can rent serious compute from a Romanian provider, in RON, with Romanian contracts. No need to wire money to AWS or have a US billing address.

Website: ai.clusterpower.com

Sources: clusterpower.com · NVIDIA Europe announcement

Provider 2 · DriverAI (Cluj-Napoca)

The 80-MW quantum-AI hybrid coming online.

What they offer: an 80-megawatt data center in Cluj integrating GPU clusters with quantum computing — the first of its kind globally if delivered as announced. Still in build-out, but positions Romania at the edge of next-gen compute.

Why it matters to you: Cluj is becoming a second AI hub alongside Bucharest. If you're flexible on geography, Cluj's startup ecosystem (UiPath co-founders are from here) is arguably tighter and more builder-focused than Bucharest's enterprise tilt.

Source: ETDatacenters coverage

Policy · EU AI Factory + Romania's national strategy

Romania was picked. That matters more than you think.

EU AI Factory: in October 2025, Romania was selected as one of six European countries to host a new AI and supercomputing center under the EU AI Factory initiative. This means subsidized compute access for Romanian startups and researchers, training programs, and a direct pipeline to EU-funded AI talent.

National AI Strategy 2024–2027: the government's plan. Budget is modest (~105M RON ≈ €21M — small vs Portugal's €400M), so don't expect state money to fund your startup. But the strategy does signal which sectors get prioritized: healthcare, public administration, education, manufacturing.

Why it matters to you: AI Factory status means grants, mentors, and free/subsidized training are coming online over the next 12 months. Watch eu.ai or Romania's Ministry of Research announcements. These are not huge — but they're free, and free is free.

Sources: OECD AI Policy Observatory · Romania tech ecosystem coverage

Provider 3 · NVIDIA's €4B commitment

The biggest signal of all.

NVIDIA announced plans to invest up to €4 billion in Romania, citing the country's energy capacity, EU membership, and engineering talent pool. The investment is reportedly tied to building data-center capacity, training programs, and partnerships with local universities.

Translation: NVIDIA thinks Romania has the bones to be a regional AI hub. When the GPU maker picks a country, that country becomes more credible to foreign investors overnight. Expect more Series A activity, more foreign VCs opening Bucharest offices, more international hires.

Sources: Romanian tech press · NVIDIA data center

What this means in one line: if you build an AI product for the Romanian market (or Romania + EU), you now have local compute, policy support, EU funding access, and global credibility on your side. That's a rare combination in Eastern Europe.
