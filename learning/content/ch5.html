The big idea: the AI is brilliant but has zero common sense. It doesn't know who you are, what you want, or how you like things formatted — unless you tell it. Your prompt is the steering wheel.

Seven rules. With before/after.

Rule 1 · Give it a role

Tell the AI who to be.

Without a role, the model averages across all possible helpful people. With a role, it locks onto a specific style and knowledge.

❌ Weak prompt
Explain how taxes work.
✓ Strong prompt
You are a friendly accountant explaining to a first-year freelancer in Romania. Use plain language. No jargon. Include one concrete example with real numbers.
Rule 2 · Be specific about format

Tell it exactly how to format the answer.

"Short answer" is vague. "Three bullet points, max 15 words each" is a contract.

❌ Weak
Give me some startup ideas.
✓ Strong
Give me 5 startup ideas in the legal-tech space. For each: one-line problem, one-line solution, target buyer. Format as a numbered list. No fluff.
Rule 3 · Show, don't just tell (few-shot)

Give it 2–3 examples of what you want.

The single biggest unlock. If you can show the model what a good answer looks like, it'll match that style almost every time.

❌ Weak
Write a friendly follow-up email to a customer.
✓ Strong
Write a friendly follow-up email after a demo. Example of the tone I want: "Hi Maria — thanks for the chat Tuesday. As discussed, attaching the proposal. Happy to walk through it Friday if useful. — Alex" Now write one for a customer named Stefan who saw our product demo yesterday and hasn't replied yet.
Rule 4 · Say what NOT to do

Negative constraints work.

Models listen to "don't" just as well as "do." Use this to kill the things you hate seeing.

✓ With constraints
Summarize this article. - 3 sentences max. - Don't use jargon. - Don't start with "In today's..." - Don't make stuff up. If something isn't in the article, say so.
Rule 5 · Chain of thought

For hard problems, ask it to think first.

Add "think step by step before answering" and the quality jumps on math, logic, planning, debugging. It uses more tokens but the answer is dramatically better.

✓ For hard problems
I'm planning a 3-day trip to Cluj with a €400 budget. Think step by step: estimate flights, accommodation, food, and activities. Then give me a final plan under budget.
Rule 6 · Iterate

Your first prompt will be wrong.

That's normal. The magic is in the second and third attempt. Read the output, see what's off, add a constraint or example, try again. Each round usually gets 2× better.

Pro tip: paste the bad output back into the prompt and say "this is wrong because X. Try again." The model takes the correction seriously.

Rule 7 · Give context the model can't see

The model doesn't know what you know.

Paste the customer's email. Quote the document. Include the actual data. Don't make the model guess — show it the source.

✓ With context
Here's the customer's last 3 emails (pasted below). Draft a reply that acknowledges their frustration about the late delivery and offers the 20% discount we discussed internally. --- [paste the emails here]
